- How does the length of the trajectories affect the training?
Short trajectories lead to more stable updates, as the calculated returns have a lower variance.
Although they contain less information about the long term consequences.
Long trajectories contain more information about the long term consequences, but produce more noisy gradients.
Errors and randomness will accumulate and lead to a more instability, so the performance will vary between runs.

- How is the sample complexity (how many steps it takes to solve the environment) of this algorithm related to the DQN from the last exercise?
DQN is more efficient, because past experiences and td-learning will be used.
However REINFORCE only uses complete trajectories once and suffers from high variance.
So REINFORCE will need more samples to learn a good policy.
